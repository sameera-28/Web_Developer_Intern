Who is A.P.J.Abdul Kalam?

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, in full "Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam", (born October 15, 1931, Rameswaram, India—died July 27, 2015, Shillong), Indian scientist and politician who played a leading role in the development of India’s missile and nuclear weapons programs. He was president of India from 2002 to 2007.Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam was a man of simplicity and integrity. He was so busy at work that he rises up early in the morning and work till late hours after midnight.

Awards of Dr A.P.J Abdul Kalam

  • 1981: Padma Bhushan – Government of India
  • 1990: Padma Vibhushan – Government of India
  • 1997: Indira Gandhi Award for National Integration – Government of India
  • 1998: Veer Savarkar Award – Government of India
  • 2000: SASTRA Ramanujan Prize – Shanmugha Arts,Science,Technology & Research Academy, India
  • 2013: Von Braun Award – National Space Society
